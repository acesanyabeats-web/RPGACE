# /debate — adversarial case-building before a real decision

Most decisions in this project get one pass of reasoning and then a verdict. That's right for most things, but it fails on a specific class of question: when there's a genuine, real tension between two positions and picking one too fast means never actually hearing the other side's strongest case. This skill exists for exactly that class of question - not a debate for its own sake, and not a rhetorical exercise, but a real attempt to find where two things actually disagree before deciding which one to trust.

**The trigger case this was built for**: comparing Claude's own general/basic training knowledge against RPGACE's real, gathered, hand-researched taxonomy tree content on the same topic. These are epistemically different things - one is broad training-data pattern-matching, the other is Alex's own studied, source-cited, human-checkpointed knowledge - and they can genuinely agree, genuinely conflict, or look similar while resting on different justifications. The whole point of running this as a debate rather than a single-pass comparison is that a single pass tends to average the two into a bland "mostly compatible" summary; forcing each side to actually attack the other surfaces the real disagreements a summary would smooth over.

## The procedure, in order

1. **Dissection pass — decide what's actually worth debating.** Before any rounds start, work out (a Claude agent doing this inline, or a dispatched agent for a large/multi-topic comparison) which specific dimensions or sub-topics genuinely have tension. Don't debate things that trivially agree. This step is what keeps the debate real instead of generic — it should name the actual points of possible friction (a specific insight, a specific claim, a specific architectural tradeoff) before assigning sides.

2. **Attack phase — both sides go first, in alternating rounds.** Side A makes its strongest attack on Side B's position (Round 1A). Side B attacks back — not defending yet, attacking Side A's position on its own terms (Round 1B). Repeat for as many rounds as the dissection pass found real material for (Round 2A/2B, etc.) — stop when new rounds stop surfacing genuinely new attack surfaces, not on a fixed count. Apply GODMODE (real evidence — cite the actual taxonomy_tree row, the actual gathered source, the actual reasoning — never a vague assertion) and a real interrogation pass (does this attack actually hold up, or does it collapse under one obvious question?) at every single round, not just at the end. A weak attack that doesn't survive its own interrogation gets dropped before the next round, not carried forward to pad the total.

3. **Defense phase — only after every attack is on the table.** Each side responds specifically to the attacks made against it in phase 2 - not a fresh restatement of its own position, an actual rebuttal to the specific point raised. This ordering matters: defending before all attacks are known produces answers to imagined objections instead of the real ones.

4. **Council of 5 cleans up both cases.** Once attack + defense are both complete, Council of 5 reviews the full transcript for quality - not to declare a winner, but to strip repetition, cut attacks that didn't survive interrogation, and produce the two strongest, most honest final cases (real risk, real cost, real "why this side might still be wrong" for each). This is the step that turns a raw adversarial transcript into something actually readable.

5. **Alex decides.** The skill's output is always both sides' cleaned-up case, presented side by side - never a recommended winner. "Full scope on 2 versions, Alex chooses truth" is the literal design: this tool's job stops at making both cases as strong and honest as they can be. If the debate concerns whether something should be added to a real system (like RPGACE's taxonomy tree), nothing gets written anywhere as a result of the debate itself - a debate "won" only means Alex was persuaded, which is a separate, explicit decision to actually build something, made the normal way (Council of 5 → Omnitrix, same as any other feature).

## Guardrails

- Never let the debate itself write to a database, insert a taxonomy node, or otherwise change RPGACE - it produces a comparison, not a commit. Building on a debate's outcome is a separate, deliberate follow-up step with its own normal process.
- Don't debate things that don't have real tension - if the dissection pass can't find genuine friction, say so plainly instead of manufacturing a fight for two sides that mostly agree.
- Keep each side's attacks grounded in real, checkable material (an actual row, an actual quote, an actual citation) - an unfalsifiable attack ("this just feels less rigorous") doesn't survive the interrogation step and shouldn't reach Council of 5.
